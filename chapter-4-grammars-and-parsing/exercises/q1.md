### Exercise 1

Each sentence is ambiguous because it can be interpreted in more than one way. The ambiguity arises from different possible parse trees and grammatical structures.

#### (a) I saw an elephant in my pajamas.

**Meaning 1:**

```txt
I was wearing pajamas when I saw an elephant.
```

**Meaning 2:**

```txt
The elephant was in my pajamas.
```

**Reason for Ambiguity:**

The prepositional phrase:

```txt
in my pajamas
```

can modify either:

```txt
saw
```

or:

```txt
elephant
```

---

#### (b) I cannot recommend this student too highly.

**Meaning 1:**

```txt
I strongly recommend this student.
```

**Meaning 2:**

```txt
I cannot honestly give this student a very high recommendation.
```

**Reason for Ambiguity:**

The phrase:

```txt
too highly
```

can be interpreted either as strong praise or as an upper limit on the recommendation.

---

#### (c) I saw her duck.

**Meaning 1:**

```txt
I saw the duck that belongs to her.
```

Here:

```txt
duck = noun
```

**Meaning 2:**

```txt
I saw her lower her head or body quickly.
```

Here:

```txt
duck = verb
```

**Reason for Ambiguity:**

The word:

```txt
duck
```

can function as either a noun or a verb.

---

#### (d) Students avoid boring professors.

**Meaning 1:**

```txt
Students avoid professors who are boring.
```

Here:

```txt
boring = adjective
```

**Meaning 2:**

```txt
Students avoid making professors bored.
```

Here:

```txt
boring = verb (gerund)
```

**Reason for Ambiguity:**

The word:

```txt
boring
```

can act as either an adjective modifying:

```txt
professors
```

or a verb describing an activity.

---

#### (e) Milk drinkers turn to powder.

**Meaning 1:**

```txt
People who drink milk become powder.
```

**Meaning 2:**

```txt
People who drink milk switch to powdered milk.
```

**Reason for Ambiguity:**

The phrase:

```txt
turn to powder
```

can mean either:

```txt
become powder
```

or:

```txt
begin using powdered milk
```

---

### Conclusion

All five sentences are ambiguous because they allow multiple grammatical interpretations. Different parse trees produce different meanings, illustrating the concept of ambiguity in natural languages.
