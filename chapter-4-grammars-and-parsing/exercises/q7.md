### How Derivations Work

A derivation starts with the start symbol and repeatedly replaces nonterminals using grammar productions until only terminals remain.

The difference between leftmost and rightmost derivations is:

```txt
Leftmost Derivation:
Always expand the leftmost nonterminal first.

Rightmost Derivation:
Always expand the rightmost nonterminal first.
```

---

### The Grammar

```txt
Start ‚Üí E $

E ‚Üí T plus E
  | T

T ‚Üí T times F
  | F

F ‚Üí ( E )
  | num
```

Suppose we want to derive:

```txt
num plus num times num plus num $
```

---

### Step 1: Determine the Overall Structure

Before beginning the derivation, examine the target expression:

```txt
num + num * num + num
```

The outermost operator is:

```txt
plus
```

because the grammar defines:

```txt
E ‚Üí T plus E
```

Therefore the first production chosen is:

```txt
E ‚áí T plus E
```

which gives:

```txt
T plus E
```

---

### Leftmost Derivation Algorithm

```txt
repeat until only terminals remain

1. Find the leftmost nonterminal.
2. Choose an appropriate production.
3. Replace that nonterminal.
4. Repeat.
```

---

### Example Leftmost Derivation

```txt
Start
‚áí E $
‚áí T plus E $
‚áí F plus E $
‚áí num plus E $
‚áí num plus T plus E $
‚áí num plus T times F plus E $
‚áí num plus F times F plus E $
‚áí num plus num times F plus E $
‚áí num plus num times num plus E $
‚áí num plus num times num plus T $
‚áí num plus num times num plus F $
‚áí num plus num times num plus num $
```

At every step, the leftmost nonterminal is expanded.

---

### Rightmost Derivation Algorithm

```txt
repeat until only terminals remain

1. Find the rightmost nonterminal.
2. Choose an appropriate production.
3. Replace that nonterminal.
4. Repeat.
```

---

### Example Rightmost Derivation

For:

```txt
num times num plus num times num $
```

```txt
Start
‚áí E $
‚áí T plus E $
‚áí T plus T $
‚áí T plus T times F $
‚áí T plus T times num $
‚áí T plus F times num $
‚áí T plus num times num $
‚áí T times F plus num times num $
‚áí T times num plus num times num $
‚áí F times num plus num times num $
‚áí num times num plus num times num $
```

At every step, the rightmost nonterminal is expanded.

---

### How Do We Choose Productions?

The key idea is to look at the target string and determine its structure.

For example:

```txt
num + num * num + num
```

contains:

```txt
+
+
*
```

The outermost structure is:

```txt
num + (...)
```

Therefore we begin with:

```txt
E ‚áí T plus E
```

Then we recursively derive the remaining portion of the expression.

---

### What a Parser Does

A derivation generates a string from the grammar:

```txt
Start Symbol
     ‚Üì
Input String
```

A parser performs the reverse process:

```txt
Input String
     ‚Üì
Parse Tree
```

When solving derivation exercises, you are manually simulating how the grammar generates a valid sentence in the language.
