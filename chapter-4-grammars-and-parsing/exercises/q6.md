### Exercise 6

Constructing a grammar whose generated strings are decimal representations of irrational numbers is difficult for several reasons.

First, irrational numbers have **infinite, non-terminating decimal expansions**. Examples include:

```txt
π  = 3.141592653589793...
√2 = 1.414213562373095...
e  = 2.718281828459045...
```

A context-free grammar generates only **finite strings**, whereas the decimal representation of an irrational number is infinite. Therefore, a CFG cannot directly generate the complete decimal expansion of an irrational number.

Another difficulty is that irrational numbers are defined by what they are **not**:

```txt
irrational numbers = real numbers that are not rational
```

To determine whether a decimal representation is irrational, one must verify that the digits do not eventually become periodic. For example:

```txt
1/3 = 0.333333...
1/7 = 0.142857142857...
```

These are rational numbers because their decimal expansions eventually repeat.

A grammar would therefore need to distinguish:

```txt
non-repeating infinite decimal expansions
```

from:

```txt
repeating infinite decimal expansions
```

Detecting the absence of repetition is not something that can be expressed naturally by a context-free grammar.

Furthermore, there are infinitely many irrational numbers, and most have no simple pattern in their decimal expansions. A grammar would need to encode an infinite amount of information to generate exactly all irrational numbers and exclude all rational numbers.

Therefore, the main difficulties are:

1. Irrational numbers have infinite decimal representations, while CFGs generate finite strings.
2. Irrationality depends on the absence of eventual repetition.
3. Determining non-periodicity is beyond the capabilities of ordinary CFGs.
4. Most irrational numbers have no finite, predictable pattern that can be described by a grammar.

Hence, constructing a CFG whose language consists exactly of the decimal representations of irrational numbers is not practical and, in general, cannot be done directly.
