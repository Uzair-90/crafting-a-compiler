### **Question No. 11**

The Measure Of Software Similarity (MOSS) [SWA03] tool can detect
similarity of programs written in a variety of modern programming lan-
guages. Its main application has been in detecting similarity of programs
submitted in computer science classes, where such similarity may indi-
cate plagiarism (students, beware!). In theory, detecting equivalence of
two programs is undecidable, but MOSS does a very good job of ﬁnding
similarity in spite of that limitation.
Investigate the techniques MOSS uses to ﬁnd similarity. How does MOSS
diﬀer from other approaches for detecting possible plagiarism?

### **Solution**

MOSS detects program similarity by converting source code into token sequences, normalizing identifiers and syntax, and then computing overlapping k-gram fingerprints that are hashed into compact representations. Similarity is measured by comparing shared fingerprints between programs, allowing it to detect structural similarity even when variable names or formatting have been changed. Unlike text-based diff tools, MOSS is robust to superficial modifications, and unlike semantic analysis or theorem-proving approaches, it does not attempt to determine program equivalence, making it scalable and efficient. Compared to AST-based or semantic methods, MOSS trades deep program understanding for efficiency and robustness against common plagiarism techniques such as renaming, reformatting, and code reordering, making it particularly effective for large-scale academic plagiarism detection.
